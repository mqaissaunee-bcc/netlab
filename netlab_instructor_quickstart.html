Welcome & Quick Navigation Your streamlined guide to teaching with NETLAB+ VE

This Quick Start Guide is designed for instructors who have been assigned to a course with pre-populated content on NETLAB+ Virtual Edition. Your class, content, and student accounts have already been set up by your administrator or lead instructor.

What this guide covers: Logging in, navigating the interface, managing your class roster, scheduling and entering lab reservations, and viewing usage reports. Advanced features like creating classes, designing pods/labs, managing accounts, and configuring content are covered in the full Instructor Guide.

1. Initial Login Access NETLAB+ for the first time and configure your account

Your NETLAB+ administrator will provide you with the URL or IP address of the NETLAB+ system, along with a username and initial password.

1

Open a web browser and navigate to the URL or IP address of your NETLAB+ system. Supported browsers include Google Chrome (recommended), Mozilla Firefox, Apple Safari (Mac), and Microsoft Edge.

Cookies and JavaScript must be enabled in your browser. Using the most recent version of your browser is recommended.
2

Enter your Username and Password into the login fields, then click the Login button.

3

On your first login, you will be required to change your password. NETLAB+ enforces strong passwords that must be 7 or more characters, contain both letters and numbers, and not be a common dictionary word.

Password tip: Combine a word with numbers, for example “airplane789”. You may not reuse your initial password.
4

You may optionally update your email address. This step can be skipped during initial login.

5

Configure your Date and Time Settings. These are critical for the lab scheduler to display times correctly.

When traveling, change your time zone to match the local time. NETLAB+ adjusts for Daylight Savings Time automatically.

2. MyNETLAB Interface Navigate the home page, settings, and key menu options

After a successful login, the MyNETLAB page is displayed. This is your home page. Any scheduled lab reservations will appear here, along with the option to create a New Lab Reservation.

Key menu areas for instructors: The Schedule menu is used to schedule and manage lab reservations. The Manage menu provides access to class rosters and usage reporting. Your username menu (top-right) contains Settings and Logout.

Modify User Settings

Click your username in the top-right corner and select Settings to access your account settings page. From here you can change your password, update your email, or adjust date and time display preferences.

Logout

Click your username in the top-right corner and select Logout. A confirmation message will display with the option to Log In Again.

Logging out is especially important on shared computers to prevent unauthorized access to your account.

3. Manage Your Class Roster Add or drop learners, create teams, reset passwords, and view student details

As a lead instructor, you can manage the learners (students and other users) enrolled in your class. Access class management by clicking Manage in the top menu, then selecting Classes, and clicking on your class name.

Add Learners to the Class Roster

1

Select the Roster tab on the class page and click Add Learners.

2

A list of user accounts in the community will be displayed. Check the Select boxes for the learners you want to add and click Add Selected.

Drop Learners from the Roster

Select the Drop from Roster option on the Action dropdown for the learner you want to remove. You will be prompted to confirm. To drop multiple learners at once, check multiple Select boxes, click Selected Items, then choose Drop from Roster.

Dropping a learner from the roster does not delete their user account. The account remains on the system and can be added to other classes.

View User Account Details & Labs

Click the Action dropdown on any roster member and select View User Account to see their details, or View User Labs to see the labs completed by that learner for the class.

Group the Class Roster Into Teams

Teams allow students to share lab reservations and work in groups. To assign teams, select the learners using the checkboxes, click Selected Items, then Add to Team. Choose a team letter (A through Z) and click Submit.

Lead instructors are not assigned to a team but may participate in the lab reservations for any team. The class “self-study lab access” setting must be enabled for team reservations.

Reset Account Passwords for Learners

To reset passwords for one or more learners, select the learners using the checkboxes, click Selected Items, then Reset Password. Enter a temporary password (7+ characters, letters and numbers). The learner will be required to change it on their next login.

4. Schedule Lab Reservations Instructor-led training, student labs, team labs, and personal use

NETLAB+ offers several scheduling options, accessible from the Schedule menu on the top menu bar or by clicking the New Lab Reservation button on MyNETLAB.

Scheduling options available to you:
• Schedule Instructor-Led Training (ILT) — You and the entire class share the same reservation
• Schedule Lab for Myself — Personal practice on lab equipment
• Schedule Lab for a Student — Assign a specific student to a lab exercise
• Schedule Lab for a Team — A team shares the lab reservation
• View or Cancel Reservations — Manage existing reservations

Schedule Instructor-Led Training (ILT)

This is the most common option for teaching. You and your entire class will share the same lab reservation.

1

Click Schedule > Schedule Instructor-Led Training.

2

Select the class that will be using the lab by clicking anywhere on the row. If you lead only one class, you will skip this step.

3

A list of lab exercises associated with the class will appear. Click the lab name to select it. You may also use the search box in the upper-right to find a specific lab, or use the Action dropdown > Preview Lab to preview the content.

4

The Pod Scheduler will be displayed. Use the calendar (upper-left) to select a date. The schedule table shows available pods as columns and time slots as rows. The red line indicates the current time.

5

Click an available time slot below the red line. Review the reservation details, adjust the End Time if needed, and click Submit. A confirmation message will appear.

The last 10 minutes of a lab reservation are used for system cleanup and reset. A 1-hour time slot provides 50 minutes of lab time. Time limits are not enforced for instructor-led training reservations.
6

The ILT reservation will appear on your MyNETLAB page and on the MyNETLAB page of every learner in the class roster, allowing them to enter and participate.

Schedule Lab for Myself

Use this option for personal practice. Select Schedule > Schedule Lab for Myself, choose a class, select a lab exercise, and pick a time on the Pod Scheduler. Only you will have access to this reservation.

Schedule Lab for a Student

Designate a specific student to work on an exercise. Select the class, then the student from the roster, then the lab exercise, and finally a time on the Pod Scheduler. As the lead instructor, you have the option of participating alongside the student.

Schedule Lab for a Team

Select the class, then the team (students must already be assigned to teams), then the lab and time. Team members share access to the lab equipment during the reservation.

View or Cancel Reservations

Select Schedule > View or Cancel Lab Reservations to see all scheduled reservations. Use the Action dropdown to View details or Cancel a reservation. You will be prompted to confirm cancellations.

5. Enter a Lab Session Navigate the lab interface, access devices, and use lab tools

At the scheduled time, an Enter Lab button will appear on your MyNETLAB page. Click it to launch the lab access interface.

You may see the Initializing POD indicator at the beginning of your session. This means NETLAB+ is preparing the lab devices. Allow the initialization to complete before proceeding.

Lab Interface Tabs

The lab access interface is divided into several tabbed sections. A Time Remaining indicator is displayed in the upper-right corner.

Tab descriptions:
• Topology — Displays the network diagram with IP addresses and connections. Click a device icon to access it.
• Content — Shows the lab instructions (PDF). You can zoom, download, or print.
• Status — Shows power state and options for all devices (Power On/Off, Send CTRL+ALT+DEL, Undock, etc.).
• Device tabs (R1, R2, S1, PC A, etc.) — Click to interact with each device directly.

Accessing & Interacting with Devices

Click on a device tab (e.g., R1) to interact with it. For routers and switches, you will see a CLI terminal where you can enter commands. Click the device tab name again to access additional options:

• Send Break Key — Simulate the Break Key sequence
• Copy Selected Text — Copy text from the terminal
• Paste to Terminal — Paste configuration text (use this instead of direct clipboard paste)
• Undock/Dock — Display the device in its own floating window
• Password Recovery — Remove console and enable passwords (instructor only in ILT)
• Load/Save/Erase Configuration — Manage device configurations
• Terminal Settings — Customize background color, text color, and font size

Pod-wide Device Automation

Use the Pod menu option at the top of the lab interface to perform actions on all devices at once: Erase All Devices, Load All Devices, or Save All Devices.

Screenshots of Remote PCs

Click on a PC tab and select Take Screenshot to capture the current state. Add an optional note and click Submit. Screenshots are saved in the lab history for review via Usage reporting. You can direct students to take screenshots to document their progress.

Extend a Reservation

If additional time is needed during a lab, click Reservation in the top menu and select Extend Reservation. Select an extended end time (subject to pod availability) and click Submit.

End a Reservation

When you are finished, click Reservation > End Reservation and confirm. This ends the session for all participants (in ILT mode). The reservation will also end automatically when the reserved time expires.

For ILT sessions: Ending the reservation ends it for the entire class, not just yourself. Confirm that all students are finished before ending.

6. Usage Reporting Track lab activity at the class, account, and lab level

Usage reporting allows you to monitor student and class activity. Access it by clicking Manage > Usage.

Class Usage

Select MyNETLAB > Classes on the Usage page. Choose your class(es), reservation type(s), and optional date range, then click Submit. The report shows Reservations Made, Labs Attended, Hours Reserved, and Hours Attended. Click any column header to sort. You can Export the data to a CSV file.

Account Usage

Select MyNETLAB > Accounts on the Usage page. This shows usage per individual user, including User ID, Name, Email, and lab activity metrics. This data can also be exported to CSV.

Lab Usage

Select MyNETLAB > Labs on the Usage page. This report shows usage at the lab exercise level, including which students completed which labs and for how long. You can aggregate results by lab and/or by account.

Lab History & Screenshots

From Lab Usage details, you can view the lab history for individual reservations, including session logs, command indexes, final configurations, and any screenshots taken during the session. This is useful for assessing student work.

Assessment tip: Direct students to take screenshots during lab sessions. These screenshots will appear in the lab history alongside session logs and device configurations, giving you a comprehensive view of their work.
