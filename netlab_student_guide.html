Initial Login

🔒 Logging In for the First Time

Your instructor or NETLAB+ administrator will provide you with login credentials. The first time you log in, you will be guided through setting up your password, email, and date/time preferences.

Navigate to your NETLAB+ system URL in your web browser. Your instructor will provide this address.

Enter your Username and Password into the login fields and click the Login button.

You will be prompted to change your password. NETLAB+ enforces strong passwords.

  • Not found in the common dictionary and not too simple
  • 7 or more ASCII characters
  • Must contain both numbers and letters
💡
Tip: Combine a word with numbers. For example, “airplane” alone would not work, but “airplane789” would.

Enter your email address (optional but recommended) and configure your date and time settings:

Choose your Time Zone, preferred Date Display Format (e.g., YYYY-MM-DD), Time Display Format (12-hour or 24-hour), and First Day of the Week. These settings are important for accurate scheduling.

ⓘ
If you travel, you can update your time zone at any time to match your local time.

MyNETLAB Interface

🏠 Your Dashboard

After logging in, you land on the MyNETLAB page. This is your home base, showing all your scheduled lab reservations and providing access to all NETLAB+ features.

You can update your password, email, and date/time settings at any time. Click your username in the top-right corner and select Settings.

From the Settings page you can:

Change Password — Enter a new password meeting the strength requirements and confirm it.
Change Email — Update your email address (must be valid format).
Date & Time Settings — Adjust time zone, date format, time format, and first day of week.

Click your username in the top-right corner and select Logout. A confirmation message will display.

⚠️
Important: Always log out when using a shared computer to prevent others from accessing your account.

Schedule a Lab Reservation

📅 Starting a New Reservation

To schedule a lab reservation, log in to NETLAB+ and you have two options:

Click the New Lab Reservation button on the MyNETLAB page.

Alternatively, click Schedule in the top menu bar. You can schedule a lab for yourself or share as a team.

📚 Select a Class and Lab Exercise

If you are enrolled in more than one class, select a class by clicking anywhere on the row.

A list of lab exercises associated with the class will appear. Click on the lab name to select it. You can preview the lab content by clicking the preview icon.

🕒 Use the Pod Scheduler

The Pod Scheduler displays available pods and time slots. Columns show available pods; rows show time slots. The solid red line indicates the current time.

Scroll the table to find available time-slots. Click a time slot below the red line to select it. If you want to start immediately, select the slot just below the red line.

💡
Tip: To start your lab now, select the time-slot immediately below the red line.

Review the reservation details. You may modify the End Time if needed. Then click Submit. A confirmation message will appear.

⚠️
Note: The last 10 minutes of a lab reservation is used for system cleanup and reset. A 1-hour time slot gives you 50 minutes of usable lab time.

✅ Reservation Confirmed

Your reservation now appears on the MyNETLAB page under Scheduled Lab Reservations. Reservations you make for yourself are visible to you and your instructor. Team reservations are visible to all team members and your instructor.

Enter a Lab Session

▶️ Joining Your Lab

At the scheduled time, the Enter Lab button will appear next to your reservation. Click it to enter the lab.

ⓘ
It may be necessary to refresh your browser to display the Enter Lab button at the scheduled time.

You may see the Initializing POD... indicator. This means NETLAB+ is preparing the lab devices. Allow this to complete.

⚠️
Click “Dismiss” only if you want to interrupt initialization and return to MyNETLAB.

🖥 The Lab Access Interface

The lab interface is divided into several tabbed sections:

Topology Tab — Shows the lab topology diagram with IP addresses and device connections. Click a device icon to jump to that device’s tab.

Content Tab — Displays the lab instructional material (PDF). Options to download and print may be available.

Status Tab — Shows the status of all devices in the pod with power state and available actions.

Device Tabs — Each device (routers, switches, PCs) has its own tab for direct access.

ⓘ
The Time Remaining indicator in the top-right shows hours and minutes left. Watch this to manage your time effectively.

Click on a device tab (e.g., R1, S1, PC A) to access that device. Click the device name again to see a dropdown with additional options:

Key options include:

Copy Selected Text — Highlight text in the terminal, then click this option to copy to clipboard.
Paste to Terminal — Opens a text window where you can paste configuration commands. Use this instead of pasting directly to avoid buffer issues.
Undock / Dock — Pop a device into its own resizable window to view multiple devices side by side. Each window has a different background color for easy identification.
Save / Load / Erase Configuration — Manage device configurations during your lab session.
Power On / Power Off — Toggle device power.
Settings — Customize terminal background color, text color, and font size.

Taking & Viewing Screenshots

📸 Taking Screenshots of Remote PCs

The Screenshot feature lets you capture screenshots of remote PCs during your lab session. These are saved in your lab history for later review.

⚠️
The Screenshot feature must be enabled by your NETLAB+ administrator. The maximum number of screenshots per lab is set by your administrator or lead instructor.

Click on the tab for the remote PC (e.g., PC A). Click the PC name again to open the dropdown and select Take Screenshot.

A popup will display the captured screenshot. Add a brief description in the Notes field (recommended), then click Submit.

A notification confirms the screenshot was saved. You can continue taking screenshots throughout your session. All screenshots are included in your lab history.

💡
Tip: Your instructor may direct you to take screenshots to document your progress. These are viewable through View > Completed Labs.

Extend a Lab Reservation

⏳ Requesting More Time

If you need additional time, you can request a 30-minute extension when your Time Remaining shows 15 minutes or less.

When the Time Remaining shows 15 minutes or less, the Request More Time option appears. You can also access it through Reservation > Request More Time.

Click the option to request the extension. A confirmation message will indicate the reservation has been extended.

⚠️
Extension restrictions:
• Cannot be made until 15 minutes or less remain.
• The next 30-minute slot must be available (not reserved by someone else).
• The number of extensions may be limited by community and class settings.
• Students cannot extend instructor-led training (ILT) reservations.
• Extensions must be specifically allowed by the administrator and instructor for your class.
7

End a Lab Reservation

⏹️ Ending Your Session

When your time expires, all connections are dropped automatically. You can also end your reservation early.

Click Reservation at the top of the page and select End Reservation Now.

Respond Yes to confirm you are finished with the pod. (Or click No to return.)

ⓘ
During instructor-led training (ILT) sessions, only the instructor can end the reservation.

If you finish a lab exercise early, you can switch to a different one without ending your reservation. Click Reservation > Change Exercise, then select a new lab from the list. This option is only available if the instructor has enabled “Multiple Labs in Same Reservation” for your class.

8

View Completed Labs

📋 Reviewing Your Lab History

After completing labs, you can review your history, including screenshots, device logs, and configuration files.

From the MyNETLAB page, click View in the menu bar and select Completed Labs.

A list of completed labs is displayed. You can sort by any column header and export the data. Click a reservation row to view detailed lab history.

The Lab History includes:

Summary Tab — Overview of the reservation details.
Devices Tab — Command Index, Session Logs, Final Configs, and User Configs for each device.
PCs Tab — View screenshots captured during the session. Select a PC from the dropdown and click thumbnails to view full images. Timestamps show when each screenshot was taken.

💡
Tip: Unattended labs are not included in the completed labs list. Make sure to attend and interact with your lab reservations.
